Part 1 - Numbers

Numbers in Mandarin Chinese are extremely logical. When counting to ten, you need to learn how to pronounce the characters below. When you got that under your belt you are almost done. Take a look:

0 零 líng

1 一 yī

2 二 èr

3 三 sān

4 四 sì

5 五 wǔ

6 六 liù

7 七 qī

8 八 bā

9 九 jiǔ

10 十 shí


When reading individual numbers there is a special rule to remember. When giving somebody your phone number or your hotel room number for example you have to pronounce "一" as yāo.

But how to move from there and upwards? It's very simple. See here how the numbers are stacked together in a logical way to distinguish each number.

11 十一 shíyī

12 十二 shí'èr

13 十三 shísān

14 十四 shísì

15 十五 shíwǔ

16 十六 shíliù

17 十七 shíqī

18 十八 shíbā

19 十九 shíjiǔ


The tens are very logical aswell.


20 二十 èrshí

30 三十 sānshí

40 四十 sìshí

50 五十 wǔshí

24 二十四 èrshísì

66 六十六 liùshíliù

99 九十九 jiǔshíjiǔ


When you are counting in hundreds you simply need to use the character 百 bǎi. When you are counting in hundreds and the middle number is 0, you need to mention the 0 with a 零 líng. See examples:

101 一百零一 yībǎi líng yī

102 一百零二 yībǎi líng èr

305 三百零五 sānbǎi líng wǔ

405 四百零五 sìbǎi líng wǔ

For numbers in the hundreds that don't have a 0 in the middle you follow these examples:

110 一百一十 yībǎi yīshí

113 一百一十三 yībǎi yīshísān

222 二百二十二 èrbǎi èrshí'èr

666 六百六十六 liùbǎi liùshíliù

In order to count in the thousands you need to use the character 千 qiān which means thousand. Remember when there is a 0 in the number you have to mention it once. Lets take a look:

1000 一千 yīqiān

2000 两千 liǎng qiān

1505 一千五百零五 yīqiān wǔbǎi líng wǔ

3333 三千三百三十三 sānqiān sānbǎi sānshísān

There are ways to count higher than that but as a beginner student it's best to digest these first and then move on to the tens of thousands later in your language learning journey.

Part 2 - Telling the time

When telling the time in Mandarin Chinese you can use numbers from 1-12 for the hours and then add a 点钟 diǎnzhōng which means o'clock. When saying it's 2 o'clock you need to use the character 两 liǎng instead of 二 èr. Lets take a look at an example:

One o'clock 一点钟 yīdiǎnzhōng

Two o'clock 两点钟 liǎng diǎnzhōng

Three o'clock 三点钟 sān diǎnzhōng

When you want to express minutes you need to use the characters 分钟 fēnzhōng. For seconds you use the character 秒 miǎo. Lets see how we can combine these characters to tell the time.

3:13 三点十三分钟 Sān diǎn shísān fēnzhōng

2:11 两点十一分钟 Liǎng diǎn shíyī fēnzhōng

6:25 六点二十五分钟 Liù diǎn èrshíwǔ fēnzhōng

For half hours you can use the method above or you can use the character 半 bàn which simply means half. See example:

2:30 两点半 Liǎng diǎn bàn

6:30 六点半 Liù diǎn bàn

9:30 九点半 Jiǔ diǎn bàn

Part 3 - Telling the date

If you think telling the time is simple then hold your horses and take a look at how simple it is to tell the date!

When you want to explain to your friend what date it is today, you can use the characters 日 Rì for expressing the day, 月 yuè for the month and 年 nián for the year.

January 一月 yī yuè

February 二月 èr yuè

March 三月 sān yuè

April 四月 sì yuè

May 五月 wǔ yuè

June 六月 liù yuè

July 七月 qī yuè

August 八月 bā yuè

September 九月 jiǔ yuè

October 十月 shí yuè

November 十一月 shí yī yuè

In Mandarin Chinese you need to tell the date by first stating the year, then the month and finally ending with the day. From big to small. Like this x年x月x日. See example:

20th september 2020 = 2020年9月20日

After finishing this lesson you should be able to tell the time and date, also count up to thousands in Mandarin Chinese with confidence. We do however recommend taking a whole week to absorb this useful knowledge and practice with your clients.
