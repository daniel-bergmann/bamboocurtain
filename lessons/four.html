Getting down to business

When starting a conversation or enjoying simple small talk with your clients it's important to know how to start.

In order to break the ice before you end up like the Titanic, you need to know basic greetings.

As this is the fourth and final lesson on our platform we can use the vocabulary we have already gone through and make a simple dialogue that we can use to practice today at the job. In this lesson, we've removed the pinyin crutches so you need to put on your game face and combat this lesson with an attitude of an emperor at times of warring states.

If you need additional help, you can look up the words in your Pleco dictionary. Let's get down to business!

Client: 你好！

You: 你好！你好吗？

Client: 我很好！你呢？

You: 我非常好！

Client: 很好！拜拜！

You: 拜拜！

In the dialogue above we can see you and your client having a simple conversation that can happen almost every day. Be it when you gather with a tour group or when you simply meet them on the street.

If you want to ask them some questions we can use the character 吗. Simply put it at the end of the sentence. See the example below:

Client: 你好！

You: 你好！你好吗？

Client: 我很好！你呢？

You: 我非常好！你去酒店吗？

Client: 我去酒店。你看书吗？

You: 我不看书，我看地图。

Client: 你没有手机吗？

You: 我没有手机。

In the dialogue below we can see how we can use 呢 instead of 吗 in the same scenario with some minor alterations.

Client: 你好！

You: 你好！你好吗？

Client: 我很好！你呢？

Client: 我去酒店。你呢？

You: 我不去酒店，我看地图。

Client: 你手机呢？

You: 我没有手机。
